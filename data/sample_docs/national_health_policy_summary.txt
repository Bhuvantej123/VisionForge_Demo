National Health Policy 2017 — Key Highlights

India's National Health Policy (NHP) 2017 sets out the government's priorities for the health sector over the next decade.

Vision
The policy envisions the highest possible level of health and well-being for all at all ages, through a preventive and promotive health care orientation in all development policies, and universal access to affordable, quality health care services without anyone having to face financial hardship.

Key Goals
- Increase life expectancy at birth from 67.5 to 70 years by 2025.
- Reduce under-five mortality to 23 per 1000 live births by 2025.
- Reduce infant mortality rate to 28 per 1000 live births by 2025.
- Reduce maternal mortality ratio to 100 per 100,000 live births by 2020.
- Reduce total fertility rate to 2.1 at national level by 2025.
- Achieve significant reduction in prevalence of communicable, non-communicable diseases.

Financing
- Increase government health expenditure to 2.5% of GDP by 2025.
- More than two-thirds of government health spending at the primary care level.
- Reduce out-of-pocket expenditure from current levels of 60% to 25% of total health expenditure by 2025.

Primary Health Care
The policy emphasises a comprehensive primary health care package including:
- Reproductive, maternal, newborn, child and adolescent health
- Communicable diseases — TB, malaria, HIV/AIDS, leprosy
- Non-communicable diseases — diabetes, hypertension, mental health, oral health
- Geriatric health, palliative care, and rehabilitative care

Digital Health
- Promote private sector participation in digital health infrastructure
- Establish national digital health authority
- Health data privacy and security framework

Human Resources
- Specialist doctors in underserved areas via National Health Mission incentives
- Increase MBBS seats, especially in under-served states
- Mid-level health providers (Health and Wellness Centres) to bridge rural gaps
