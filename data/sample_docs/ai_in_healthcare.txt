Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare — Overview

Introduction
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly transforming healthcare by enabling faster diagnosis, personalised treatment, and operational efficiency. AI applications span radiology, pathology, drug discovery, clinical decision support, and patient monitoring.

Key Application Areas

1. Medical Imaging & Diagnostics
AI algorithms, especially deep learning convolutional neural networks (CNNs), achieve radiologist-level accuracy in:
- Detecting cancerous tumours in mammograms and CT scans
- Identifying diabetic retinopathy from fundus photographs
- Classifying skin lesions as malignant or benign
Notable example: Google's DeepMind achieved 94.5% accuracy in breast cancer detection (2020 Nature study), outperforming six radiologists.

2. Drug Discovery & Development
Traditional drug discovery takes 10–15 years and costs over $2 billion per approved drug.
AI reduces this by:
- Predicting protein folding structures (AlphaFold2 — DeepMind, 2021)
- Screening millions of molecular compounds in silico
- Identifying drug repurposing candidates for rare diseases

3. Clinical Decision Support
AI-powered clinical decision support systems (CDSS):
- Alert clinicians to potential drug-drug interactions
- Flag sepsis risk in ICU patients hours earlier than standard protocols
- Recommend evidence-based treatment pathways

4. Natural Language Processing (NLP) in Healthcare
NLP extracts structured data from free-text clinical notes (EHRs), enabling:
- Automated medical coding (ICD-10)
- Patient cohort identification for research
- Summarisation of patient histories for time-poor clinicians

5. Wearables & Remote Patient Monitoring
Smartwatches and biosensors collect continuous physiological data:
- Apple Watch ECG feature detected atrial fibrillation in 0.5% of 400,000 users (Apple Heart Study, 2019)
- Continuous glucose monitoring reduces HbA1c levels in diabetic patients

Challenges
- Data privacy and HIPAA/GDPR compliance
- Algorithmic bias — models trained on non-representative populations
- Regulatory approval pathways (FDA SaMD guidelines)
- Clinician trust and explainability (black-box models)
- Integration with legacy hospital information systems

Future Outlook
By 2030, AI is expected to contribute $150 billion annually in savings to the US healthcare system alone (Accenture). The priority areas are precision medicine, mental health chatbots, and AI-assisted surgery using robotic platforms.
